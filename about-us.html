About us

Hello and Salaam everyone! We are a university working group focused on creating a safe and inclusive space, specifically for South Asian Muslims and our allies. This is meant to be an organization where students from diverse backgrounds can come together on shared Islamic and humanitarian values of equity, justice, mercy, compassion, respect, and solidarity. We welcome all Muslims, regardless of sect, country of origin, ethnicity, caste, gender, sexuality, ability, and all South Asians as well as allies. (In other words, if you identify with South Asia and/or Islam in any way, and/or are interested in learning more about Islam/the diverse cultures of the region, we welcome you to our community.

We accept and acknowledge our religious/ethnic differences in their entirety; South Asia is a vast multicultural, multilingual, and religiously diverse region with a rich history. Thus, we invite folks who may have cultural ties to Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Myanmar, Nepal, Pakistan, Sri Lanka; in communities in Fiji, the Caribbean (Grenada, Guyana, Saint Lucia, Martinique, Jamaica, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, Suriname, Trinidad, and Tobago), Mauritius, South Africa; as well as communities participating in self-determination movements in Balochistan, Kashmir, Nagaland, and elsewhere.

We came together in the fall of 2021 as an independent working group and are currently supported by Other Collective, a media platform aimed at reclaiming, amplifying, and centering marginalized Southwest Asian, South Asian, and North African identities. You can support our programming work at SAM UWG by signing up for the specific tier on our Patreon @Other Collective Media.

Our Primary goals

  • Student safety and community well-being on all college campuses
  • Building solidarity with Kashmiri self-determination movements.
  • Ensuring dialogue regarding the persecution of minority Islamic sects.
  • Holding space for LGBTQIA+ South Asian Muslim experiences.
  • Discussion and recognition of Islamic extremism within the South Asian region.
  • Offering the nuance regarding Islamophobia in the South Asian context.
  • Bringing folks into caste protections campaigns to support Dalit, Bahujan, and Adivasi community members.
